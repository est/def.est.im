take
/teɪk/
词根/词源：Old English tacan（或来自 Proto-Germanic *takwaz），原意“拿、取”。（示例占位）
字面分解：to grasp or seize — 基本动作是“伸手取得某物/占有/接受（抽象或具体）”。
首见年代：约 c. 1000（Old English 文献中已见）。

⚡ take — vtr (sth / sb)
vtr + prep
核心词汇 常用词 口语
to get into one's possession, control, or occupancy by force, skill, purchase, or favor. — （拿走；取得；接受）
其他语言注释： 西班牙语 tomar，法语 prendre（部分语义重合）。
Example: He took the book from the shelf. 译：他从书架上取下那本书。
forms: take • takes • took • taken • taking
grab
seize
pick up
antonyms: give
⚡ take — vtr (sth)
vtr
中学常考 考研/考试常见
to accept or receive (something offered). — （接受；领受）
其他语言注释： 德语 nehmen（在“接受”义项下相近）。
Example: She took the job without hesitation. 译：她毫不犹豫地接受了那份工作。
forms: take • takes • took • taken • taking
synonyms:
accept

antonyms: refuse
⚡ take — v (sth) / vtr
v (time)
日常用法 实用表达
to require as necessary or appropriate (of time, effort, etc.). — （（某事）需要（多少时间/努力））
其他语言注释： 中文常用“需要（时间）”，例如“需要三小时”。
Example: It takes about ten minutes to get there by bus. 译：坐公交去那儿大约需要十分钟。
forms: take • takes • took • taken • taking
synonyms:
require
need
antonyms: save (time)
⚡ take — phrasal / idiom
v + particle / phrasal
常见短语口语
Phrasal verbs build different meanings: e.g. take off = to leave the ground / to remove clothing; take on = to accept responsibility.
其他语言注释： 短语动词通常需逐一学习，不能直接字面翻译。
Examples:
She took off her coat. — 译：她脱下了外套。
The plane took off at noon. — 译：飞机中午起飞。
forms: take • takes • took • taken • taking
related phrases:
take off
take on
take up
antonyms: depend on phrase
