词根 · 词源 · 首次出现

From Latin inspirare "to breathe into" (in- 'into' + spirare 'to breathe'). First recorded in English late Middle English; earliest attestation c. 14th–15th century.

First attested: c. 14th century

Meanings

inspire (somebody) — v.
vtr + object
常用词 核心词汇 写作
To fill (someone) with the urge or ability to do or feel something, especially to do something creative.
中文: 激发；鼓舞；启发
"His teacher's encouragement inspired her to become a writer."
“老师的鼓励激发了她成为一名作家的热情。”
Forms: inspire, inspires, inspired, inspiring
Synonyms:
encourage motivate stimulate spark
Antonyms:
discourage dishearten
inspire (something) — v.
vtr + noun
常用词 商务
To cause (a feeling or reaction) to occur in someone; e.g. to give rise to a feeling.
中文: 使产生（某种感情或反应）
"The novel inspired great loyalty among its readers."
“那本小说激发了读者们强烈的忠诚感。”
Forms: inspire, inspired
Synonyms:
arouse evoke engender
Antonyms:
suppress
inspire — v. (archaic / literary)
v.
过时词汇 文学
(archaic) To inhale or breathe in.
中文: 吸入（过时用法）
"He inspired the sweet air of the valley."
“他吸入了山谷中甘甜的空气。”
Forms: inspire, inspired
Synonyms:
inhale

Usage Notes & Collocations

Collocations: inspire confidence, inspire fear, be inspired by, find inspiration in.

Register: widely used in both formal and informal registers; very common in writing about art, leadership and motivation.
